Introducing our Personas

M inguel

"I need better access to healthcare and education resources for my children's future."

Short description:

  • Part-time community worker.
  • 32 years old.
  • Married, two children.

Background:

Minguel lives in a remote area and works part-time in community services while raising two children. She is committed to improving her family's well-being but still facing challenges due to limited access to essential healthcare and education resources.

Needs:

  • Understand the health and education gaps affecting Indigenous families, especially in rural areas.
  • Use easy-to-read visuals and summaries to stay informed about national and local progress on key issues for her family.

Pain Points:

  • Remote location restricts medical services.
  • Struggles to find reliable learning resources.
  • Limited experience using online platforms and resources.

Technology usage:

  • Uses mobile devices occasionally for social news/information.
  • Relies on community hubs and local networks for Internet access.

A ndy

"My mother is sick. I need to find a good and affordable hospital for my mother."

Short description:

  • University professor.
  • 51 years old.
  • Lives and cares for his 73-year-old mother, who is unwell.

Background:

Andy, a 51-year-old university professor, lives with and cares for his unwell 73-year-old mother. With intermediate tech proficiency and a strong value for ethical, culturally respectful representation, he seeks clear, digestible health data to identify better regional healthcare options and advocate for improved services.

Needs:

  • Finds clear and culturally respectful data that shows how healthcare access and outcomes have changed over time.
  • Understand where life expectancy and other health-related indicators have improved to make decisions for his mother's care.
  • Access simple visual summaries that track progress in health services across regions, without needing to read through technical reports.

Pain Points:

  • Overwhelmed by dense, technical government reports.
  • Has difficulty locating data that connects national goals to real-world community impact.
  • Technology usage:
  • Comfortable using web applications for research and reporting.

K aren

"I'm tired of looking at a screen of numbers in black and white. I need a tool as a web that can motivate me to do this job."

Short description:

  • Government Policy Analyst.
  • 38 years old.
  • Lives and cares for his 73-year-old mother, who is unwell.

Background:

Karen, a 36-year-old government policy analyst, is married with two children. Tech-savvy and ambitious, she struggles with eye issues from screen time. She needs a user-friendly tool to compare 2016 and 2021 Australian census data, visualize socioeconomic progress, and export clear reports to communicate findings to policymakers.

Needs:

  • Visualize progress on selected socioeconomic outcomes with intuitive graphs and charts.
  • Export clear summaries and detailed reports quickly and efficiently.
  • Easily compare data between years from Australian censuses.

Pain Points:

  • Have eye issues due to many years looking at the screen.
  • Limited user-friendly tools for performing comparative analyses.
  • Difficulty accessing and interpreting extensive census data across different years (2016, 2021).

Technology usage:

  • Tech savvy.
  • Prefers straightforward, interactive user interfaces for exploring purposes.
